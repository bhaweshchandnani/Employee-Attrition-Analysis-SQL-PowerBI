# Employee Attrition Analysis using SQL & Power BI

## Project Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition patterns using SQL and Power BI to identify factors associated with employee turnover. The objective is to uncover attrition trends across employee demographics, workplace conditions, and career growth indicators, and provide actionable recommendations to improve employee retention.

## Dataset Overview
The dataset contains employee demographic, workplace, and career-related information along with attrition status.

## Key Insights

* Age Group
Employees aged 18–25 showed the highest attrition rate at 53%. This suggests that younger employees are more likely to leave the company compared to older age groups.

* Gender
Female employees had a higher attrition rate (53%) than male employees (42%).

* Marital Status
Single employees experienced the highest attrition rate (66%), while married employees had the lowest attrition rate (36%).

* Education Level
Attrition was fairly similar across most education levels (around 48%). However, employees with a PhD had a much lower attrition rate (24%).

* Company Tenure
Employees working in companies with 2–5 years of tenure showed the highest attrition rate (55%). Attrition generally decreased as company tenure increased.

* Job Level
Attrition was highest among entry-level employees (63%), followed by mid-level employees (45%). Senior employees had the lowest attrition rate (20%).

* Work-Life Balance
Employees reporting poor work-life balance had significantly higher attrition (60%) compared to employees with excellent work-life balance (35%).

* Remote Work
Employees without remote work options had much higher attrition (52%) than employees who worked remotely (25%).

* Promotions
Employees who received fewer promotions showed higher attrition, while employees with more promotion opportunities were less likely to leave.

## Recommendations

* Work-life balance appears to have a strong relationship with attrition. Improving flexibility and employee well-    being programs may help reduce turnover.
* Entry-level employees are leaving at a much higher rate than other groups. Better onboarding, mentoring, and        career guidance could improve retention.
* Remote employees show considerably lower attrition. Expanding remote or hybrid work options may help retain         talent.
* Employees with more promotion opportunities tend to stay longer. Creating clearer career growth paths could         improve employee retention.
* Younger and single employees represent some of the highest-risk groups. Targeted engagement and retention           initiatives for these employees may be beneficial.
