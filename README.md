# Employee Attrition Analysis using SQL & Power BI

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition patterns using SQL and Power BI to identify factors associated with employee turnover. The objective is to uncover attrition trends across employee demographics, workplace conditions, and career growth indicators, and provide actionable recommendations to improve employee retention.

## 📌 Key Insights

### Age Group

Employees aged **18–25** have the highest attrition rate (**53%**), indicating higher turnover among early-career employees.

### Gender

Female employees show a higher attrition rate (**53%**) compared to male employees (**42%**).

### Marital Status

Single employees experience the highest attrition rate (**66%**), followed by divorced employees (**40%**) and married employees (**36%**).

### Education Level

Most education groups have attrition rates around **48%**, while employees with PhD degrees show significantly lower attrition (**24%**).

### Company Tenure

Companies with **2–5 years of tenure** show the highest attrition rate (**55%**). Attrition generally decreases as company maturity increases.

### Job Level

Entry-level employees have the highest attrition rate (**63%**), followed by mid-level employees (**45%**). Senior-level employees show the lowest attrition (**20%**).

### Work-Life Balance

Employees reporting poor work-life balance have the highest attrition (**60%**), while employees with excellent work-life balance have the lowest attrition (**35%**).

### Remote Work

Employees without remote work options show significantly higher attrition (**52%**) compared to remote employees (**25%**).

### Promotions

Employees with fewer promotions (0–2 promotions) show higher attrition (**47%**) than employees receiving greater career advancement opportunities (~25%).

---

## 💡 Recommendations

- Improve work-life balance initiatives.
- Strengthen retention programs for entry-level employees.
- Expand remote and hybrid work opportunities.
- Provide clearer career growth and promotion pathways.
- Focus retention efforts on younger and single employees.
